Privacy Policy

Last updated: 16 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the Qiyam app ("the app", "we") handles your information. In short: Qiyam does not collect, store, or share your personal data. There are no user accounts, no advertising, and no third-party analytics or tracking.

Information we do not collect

Qiyam does not require you to create an account or provide any personal information such as your name, email address, or phone number. We do not use advertising identifiers, and we do not include any third-party analytics, tracking, or advertising software.

Location

If you enable prayer times or the Qibla compass, the app uses your device's location to calculate accurate prayer times and the direction of the Kaaba. This calculation happens entirely on your device. Your location is not transmitted to us or to any third party, and it is not stored on any server.

You can grant or revoke location access at any time in the iOS Settings > Privacy & Security > Location Services.

Data stored on your device

Your reading plans, progress, streaks, bookmarks, and preferences are stored locally on your device only. This information is not sent to us. Deleting the app removes this data from your device.

Downloaded content

To provide Quran audio, word-by-word data, and fonts, the app downloads content from third-party content-delivery services, including mp3quran.net, qurancdn.com, and Cloudflare. When your device requests these files, the request is handled by those providers, who may process technical information such as your IP address as part of normal internet operation. We do not control these services; please refer to their respective privacy policies. The app sends no personal information of yours in these requests.

Children's privacy

Qiyam does not collect personal information from anyone, including children. The app is suitable for users of all ages.

Changes to this policy

If we make changes to this policy, we will update the date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
